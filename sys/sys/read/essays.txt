Essay 1 – Aliens Landed in Earth
Prompt: Aliens have landed on Earth! They are defenseless and landed in a cylinder. They speak their own language, and one is holding a car tire. How do you talk to them? Be as creative as you can!
Parameters:
•	Each chapter must have at least 2 paragraphs.
•	Each paragraph must be at least 4-5 sentences.
•	Must be written in a clear and effective way.
•	Must match prompt.
•	Must answer correctly.













Essay:
CH: I: Context Clues Put to Use
	To talk to these extraterrestrial creatures, you’d first need to consider that they speak their own language. If they are holding a car tire, and walking around to humans, saying the same thing, then that word they are saying would most likely be “trade,” since they are holding a tire.
Just like when explorers just colonized the North American Continent, they were confronted with American Indians. They did not know how to speak their language, but through objects, they learned how to trade. One side (Let’s say they spoke English), would say, “Trade” to trade with the American Indians. The other side, (Let’s say they spoke Navajo), would say, “N’iini.’” (Pronounced Nee-A-Nee. Language/word for trade proven and researched). The settlers quickly found out that “n’iini’” meant “trade,” because whenever they traded with the American Indians, they said, “N’iini.’” But soon, they would find out that trade, and n’iini’ mans the same thing, so one party (let’s say English) would learn to say, “N’iini,’” so the other party (the Navajos), would understand, or vise-versa. This can be called “utilizing context clues.”
We can apply this same concept to the aliens. Let’s say they speak Glorb. (Made up language). The word they say for trade is “Glorby.” If we speak English, it’s still “Trade,” so, we will need to learn to pronounce, “Glroby.” But mistakes can be imminent. To avoid this, for this instance, it will be a perfect world where, “Glroby” means Trade. This is like using context clues to find out what you don’t know.
